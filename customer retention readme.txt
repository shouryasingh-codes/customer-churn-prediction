FOUNDER INSIGHTS
Project: Customer Retention AI System

1) Complaints are the biggest driver of churn
Customers with a high number of complaints are leaving faster than any other segment.
Improving support response time and resolution quality will directly reduce churn.

2) New users (low tenure) churn the most
Customers in their first 1–3 months show the highest churn rate.
Stronger onboarding and early engagement can significantly reduce early churn.

3) Low usage strongly predicts churn
Users with low usage frequency and short session duration tend to lose interest quickly.
Regular engagement nudges, reminders, and product education are required.

4) Missed payments almost double churn risk
Customers with payment failures have nearly twice the churn probability.
Payment reminders, easy retry options, and flexible payment methods can save these users.

5) High monthly charges increase churn
Price-sensitive customers on higher plans churn more frequently.
Offering discounts, flexible pricing, or downgrade options can improve retention.

6) Multi-product users rarely churn
Customers using two or more products show very high retention.
This segment is ideal for upselling and cross-selling strategies.

Founder Summary:
Churn is primarily driven by poor support experience, weak early engagement, low usage,
payment failures, and pricing pressure. Fixing these areas can significantly reduce churn
and increase customer lifetime value.
